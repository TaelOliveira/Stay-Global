What to study?

You probably already have some idea of what you would like to study. But there are a number of courses and schools for you to know a little more before making your decision.

Check it out on the website: https://cricos.education.gov.au/Course/CourseSearch.aspx

Choose the state you want to study, the name and type of course and click on "start search". When you see the course that interests you click on it to open the details: school information, course time and price. See how simple it is. Sometimes the site is not updated when schools offer promotional prices. The best way is to schedule a free consultation with our team.

How much will it cost?

Stay Global does not charge any fees for consulting and applying for your visa. This would cost the same price as if you applied it yourself. The difference is that with us you have more security in the process and our counselor will present you with several options for schools that suit your needs. There will be four fees in your quote.

Visa application fee:
This is the fee that we need to pay to immigration to apply for a visa.

  • Single: $620
  • Family: $1080 + $150 per dependent applicant (under 18)
  • Extra fee: $700, this additional fee will be paid after your third visa application.

    • Check the information, on Immigration website. https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/fees-and-charges/current-visa-pricing/study

    School fee:
    You usually need to pay for the first 3 months of the course + enroll + material fee, but each school has its own policy.

    English courses:
    Each week of the course is around $300, although it should be noted that the longer the course, the cheaper it can be.

  • VET Professional Courses: Three months can cost you approximately $1,500.
  • VET Professional Courses in demand: Three months can cost you approximately $3,000.
  • University degree (between AUD36,000 and $ 58,000 the full course), master's degree (between $33,000 and $58,000) or doctorate (between $45,000 and $60,000 the full course).

    • OSHC:
    Student health coverage abroad is a mandatory health insurance product required by international students studying in Australia. The cost is calculated based on the length of your stay. There are some companies that offer the OSHC, ask a quote to know the better price to your case. See an example, based on 12 months visa:

  • Single: $479
  • Couple: $2,120
  • Family: $3,091

    • Medical Examination:
    Immigration requires you to do a health check before granting your visa. This exam is valid for one year. Below is a base price, but this amount may vary depending on the local exam and immigration request.

  • Single: $321.20
  • Couple: $642,40
  • Family: $642,40 + $232.40

    • Credit card payments attract a 0.495% surcharge.

    What is the documents I need?

    Australia's application is a little simpler than offshore. Remember that we are here to assist you and that applying for a visa with us will cost you the same amount as doing it yourself. Come and talk to us, without any commitment so that you have a specialized assistance with an experienced team.
    Here are the standard documents needed:

  • Valid Passport
  • Document of the last complete degree in your country
  • Certificate of courses taken in Australia
  • English Language Proficiency proof (if it is the case)
  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)
  • OSHC: Overseas Student Health Cover, health insurance for the proposed duration of your visa see student visa grant periods. Health insurance must be Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) covering everyone included in your application.
  • Certificate Evidence you have enough money for your stay. If the document checklist indicates you must provide evidence of your financial capacity, attach the evidence to your visa application. Please check with our counselor for more information according to your country level.
  • Genuine temporary entrant documents. Every Student visa applicant must provide evidence they are a genuine temporary entrant (GTE). This is your letter of intent.
  • The application questionnaire completed.

    • If you ar applying as couple, you also should povide:

  • Marriage Certificate (or relationship certificate about 1 year);
  • Identity documents for each
  • OSHC Couple

    • If it is the first time you are applying as couple, you also need to provide:

  • Documents as relationship evidence (joint account, lease agreement, letter from witnesses - Australian Citizens, bills at the same address (bank account same address), photos);
  • Immigration form 919;

    • If you are apllying as FAMILY, you also need to provide:

  • Identity documents for each: passport and copies of birth certificates or the family book showing the names of both parents of all your dependent children. Copies of the adoption paper, if applicable. - Form 1229 Consent form to grant an Australian visa to a child under the age of 18 years.
  • Evidence of school enrolment: you must give the evidence of education for any dependant child who has turned 5, but has not turned 18 years.
  • OSHC Family

    • You may need to provide more documents. Book a free consultation with us or check all the documentation on the Immigration website (https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/student-500#HowTo)
