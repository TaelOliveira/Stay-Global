Why Australia?

Study at globally ranked institutions

Australia offers a diverse range of study options for international students, such as English courses, vocational education and training (VET) and technical and higher education (TAFE), as well as undergraduate (bachelor's) and postgraduate programs. It will certainly be a differential to have an international course in one of the ranked schools in your curriculum.

Study and work

When you have a student visa, you are allowing to work 40 hours per fortnight (fortnight means 14 days starting on a Monday) and more than 40 hours on your vacation. Most students pay their expenses (accommodation and food) by working here. Often, they also make extra money to travel around Asia and Australia. Australia is full of job opportunities in several areas.

Amazing experience

You will expand your experience here and have more time to discover beautiful beaches and places, travel and have fun with your friends. At the same time, studying and developing your career

Where to live in Australia?

Choosing where to live when moving to a new country is an important decision. There are some factors that you need to take into account, such as: schools. types of courses, ease of finding jobs and lifestyle. We have compiled this information for you with some of the best places to live in Australia to help you make an informed decision.

Sydney has an amazing looking harbour where you can find many world-class restaurants with the perfect addition of the Sydney Opera House and the Harbour bridge.     • What is the cost of living?

    As a general guideline, the international student will spend an average of AUD $ 1,753 per month (approximately $404 per week) on accommodation, food, clothing, entertainment, transportation, and telephone.

    The accommodation will be the main cost to live in here. There are some types of accommodation you can find in Australia, and the cost and availability of accommodation depends on the type you choose, some examples:

    Single room

    Many students prefer to have their privacy and a more suitable space to study.

  • Cost

    A single room for you in a shared house can cost between $250 to $400 per week. Obviously, it depends on the location chosen. Typically, you will need to pay two week advance.

  • Bills

    Electricity, gas, and water bills are usually included. Most of the time, the INTERNET is also included.

  • BONDE

    Is a security payment required in the case of damaged goods; it will returns to you at the end of the contract if nothing happened. They usually cost about 2 or 4 weeks of rent, which you will need to pay in advance.

    • Shared room

    This is the option most chosen by international students. Firstly because it is more accessible and also because it is a great opportunity to make friends and learn more about life in Australia. Often it is your flatmates who will refer you to a job and/or help you with information.

  • Cost

    A room for two can cost between $180 to $250 per week, while a room for four is around $150 to $195. Obviously, it depends on the location chosen. Usually, you will need to pay two week advance.

  • Bills

    Electricity, gas, and water bills are usually included. Most of the time, the INTERNET is also included.

  • BONDE

    Is a security payment required in the case of damaged goods; it will returns to you at the end of the contract if nothing happened. They usually cost about 2 weeks of rent, which you will need to be paid in advance.

    • Hostels and guesthouses

    Many students use hostels and guesthouses as temporary accommodation. This accommodation, usually cost around $200 to $325 per week.

    Homestay

    Homestay accommodation gives international students the option of living with an Australian family in their home. Single or shared rooms may be available, and costs vary according to the type of room (usually around AUD$235 to $325 per week). Meals are usually included.

    Rent an apartment

    Some people prefer rent a property by themselves or share the cost of a rental property with housemates. Rental agreements usually require payment of rent in advance, as well as a security payment called a bonde (usually one month's rent) that is paid up-front. You will also need to pay bills for electricity, gas and water. On average, rental accommodation costs around AUD $400 to $800 per week depends on the location and number of rooms. It will also requires you to provide some documents as :

  • Reference letters
  • Proof of income (recent payslips, bank statements, tax return)
  • Letter of employment (if you’re starting a new job)
  • Valid ID (passport, driver’s licence)
  • Resume of your rental/employment history
  • Deposit/rental ledger (summary of your rental payment history)
  • Cover letter
  • Completed application form.
